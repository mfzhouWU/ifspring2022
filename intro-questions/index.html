  5. Unlike paper, you have to account for how the screen can shift in size. The design should look good when the window is minimized, maximized and all the in-betweens. This also applies when viewed on a different device (like a phone).
  6. I think the Collusion website exemplifies effective design. It has an aesthetic, but is easy to use and navigate. I like the way they use orientation to add some flair, such as the contrast of horizontal and vertical text and the staggered photos. The color palette and boxiness also tie the design together.
  7. I think the Riot Games website exemplifies effective communication since it acts as a hub. It has links related to all their activities. Although their website contains a lot of information, what is presented to you isn't overwhelming and the home page gives you a good overview about who they are and what they do.
  8. I find Twitch to be a website that works well. They have a lot of different categories and display many types of streams on their front page, including those from smaller communities. Their recommendations make it easy to find streamers and topics that you might like.
